# Rotary-spectrophotometer
Designing a scheme of spectrophotometer which uses a rotor with difractional grating instead of regular monochromator. 
# Motivation
I became interested in that technology after advanced chemistry lesson. I found it easier than others, such as NMR or IR spectroscopy. Additionally, that method allows to measure concentrations of chemicals simultaneously, without making chemical titration. That's why I decided to make one at home. Usually classic monochromator is used in spectrophotometers. It has a complicated structure which is practically unreal to make in home contitions. 
# Research question
The main task is to make a properly functioning double beam spectrophotometer at home. In order to make the construction easier I decided to use rotor with diffractional grating. The electical part of this device will be made without major changes using opensource schemes. 
# Methods
The main property of the spectrophotometer would be its resolution. To compare it with sertificated device  I will test it by geting spectra of some common solutions, and then calculate standart deviavation of the results.

# Limitations 
One of the main problems of rotor with diffractional grating is its efficency. There would be two narrow slits on the way of light. On the one hand, if they are narrower the device will demonstrate higher frequency resolution. On another, there will be significant losses of light energy which may lead to lower absorbtion resolution of spectra.
